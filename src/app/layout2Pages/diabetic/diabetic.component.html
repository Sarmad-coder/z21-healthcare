Loss of sensation can be caught early by sensors Tissue stiffness is a reasonable proxy for loss of sensation in diabetics. This stiffness, if detected early, can save patients from the possibility of amputation in the future. We have developed an innovative diabetic neuropathy device, designed using sensors to identify and monitor tissue stiffness at early stages. The device has virtually no known competition.

Diabetic
Neuropathy

Regular monitoring to prevent amputations India has one of the largest populations of diabetics in the world. Uncontrolled high levels of blood sugar lead to loss of sensation, foot ulcers and eventually to amputation of the foot. This occurs in about 1.5 million patients each year. Regular monitoring can reduce amputations by upto 40% each year. Our diabetic neuropathy device makes such monitoring possible.
